# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People who reflexively reach for Instagram/TikTok and want to break the doomscroll habit, but have already burned out on punitive screen-time blockers. They're visiting this site after hearing about the app (word of mouth, App Store search, social) and deciding whether to download. They're skeptical of "another screen time app" and need to immediately understand the differentiator.

## Product Purpose

EarlyRise is an iOS app that makes you earn your scroll time. Instead of hard-blocking apps like Instagram/TikTok, it intercepts the launch with a pause screen and a small real-world task (drink water, 10 pushups, step outside). Completing the task unlocks the feed. No shame, no hard locks — positive reinforcement instead of restriction. The website's job is to convert visitors into App Store downloads by making this distinction unmistakable in the first few seconds.

## Brand Personality

Calm & optimistic, minimal & premium. Confident without being loud. Should feel like a deep breath at sunrise, not a productivity-bro hustle app and not a clinical wellness app. Warmth comes from the sunrise/morning metaphor and tone of voice, not from cute illustration or gimmicks.

## Anti-references

Explicitly not: Opal, Freedom, and other shame-based screen-time blockers — their clinical, restrictive, lock-you-out visual language is the exact opposite of what EarlyRise stands for. Also avoid generic SaaS-template landing pages (stock gradients, interchangeable layouts) and loud/gimmicky app marketing pages.

## Design Principles

- Show, don't tell: the "earn it, don't block it" mechanic should be demonstrated visually (product UI, the pause-screen moment) before it's explained in copy.
- Calm confidence over hustle-bro energy: no aggressive countdown timers, no guilt-tripping stats, no red alert colors.
- The sunrise is the spine: every section can nod to the dawn-breaking metaphor (light gradually winning over dark) without becoming twee.
- One unmistakable differentiator per fold: a visitor skimming should get "earn, not block" within 3 seconds of landing.
- Premium minimalism, not bare minimalism: restraint in palette and layout, but with real craft in typography, motion, and detail — not empty whitespace.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large/bold text ≥3:1, respects prefers-reduced-motion, no information conveyed by color alone.
